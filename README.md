# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Business Problem Statement

A leading retail company wants to better understand its customers’ shopping behavior to improve sales, customer satisfaction, and long-term loyalty.

Management has observed shifts in purchasing patterns across demographics, product categories, and sales channels (online vs. offline). They want to identify which factors—such as discounts, reviews, seasons, and payment preferences—most influence purchasing decisions and repeat purchases.

The main business question guiding this analysis is:

**How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?**

---

# Project Overview

This project analyzes **customer shopping behavior** using transactional data from **3,900 purchases across multiple product categories**.

The objective is to uncover insights into:

* Customer spending patterns
* Product preferences
* Customer segments
* Subscription behavior
* Discount impact on purchasing decisions

These insights help guide **data-driven business strategies** for marketing, product positioning, and customer retention.

# Project Workflow

### 1️⃣ Data Cleaning & Preparation (Python)

Data preparation and exploration were performed using **Python and Pandas**.

Steps included:

* Importing dataset using `pandas`
* Inspecting dataset structure with `df.info()`
* Generating summary statistics using `df.describe()`
* Handling missing values
* Standardizing column names to **snake_case**
* Feature engineering
* Preparing dataset for SQL analysis

---

### Missing Data Handling

Missing values in the **Review Rating** column were filled using the **median review rating for each product category**.

---

### Feature Engineering

New features created:

**age_group**

* Customers grouped into age brackets for segmentation.

**purchase_frequency_days**

* Derived metric to measure purchase behavior frequency.

---

### Data Consistency Check

`discount_applied` and `promo_code_used` contained redundant information.

To maintain a clean dataset, **promo_code_used was removed**.

---

### Database Integration

The cleaned dataset was exported to **PostgreSQL** for structured SQL analysis.

---

# Data Analysis Using SQL

SQL queries were written to answer key business questions.

### Key Business Insights

**1. Revenue by Gender**
Compared total revenue generated by male vs female customers.

**2. High-Spending Discount Users**
Identified customers who used discounts but still spent above average.

**3. Top 5 Products by Rating**
Products with the highest average review ratings.

**4. Shipping Type Comparison**
Compared average purchase amount between **Standard** and **Express shipping**.

**5. Subscribers vs Non-Subscribers**
Compared revenue contribution and spending behavior.

**6. Discount-Dependent Products**
Identified products frequently purchased with discounts.

**7. Customer Segmentation**
Customers categorized into:

* New
* Returning
* Loyal

based on purchase history.

**8. Top 3 Products per Category**
Most purchased products within each category.

**9. Repeat Buyers & Subscription Trends**
Analyzed whether customers with **more than 5 purchases** are more likely to subscribe.

**10. Revenue by Age Group**
Measured which age groups contribute the most revenue.

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategies are recommended:

### Boost Subscriptions

Promote exclusive benefits and offers to encourage customers to subscribe.

### Customer Loyalty Programs

Reward repeat buyers to move them into the **Loyal customer segment**.

### Optimize Discount Strategy

Ensure discounts boost sales without significantly affecting profit margins.

### Product Positioning

Highlight **top-rated and best-selling products** in marketing campaigns.

### Targeted Marketing

Focus marketing efforts on:

* High-revenue age groups
* Customers who prefer **express shipping**
